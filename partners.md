# Strategic Partners & Supply Chain

## Overview

The aquaculture project is designed as an integrated offshore fish farming operation supported by a strong network of strategic partners across the supply chain.

These partnerships are essential to ensure operational efficiency, cost optimization, risk reduction, and successful market access.

---

## Supply Chain Structure

The project supply chain includes five main components:

1. Fingerling (juvenile fish) supply  
2. Feed supply  
3. Farming infrastructure & equipment  
4. Logistics & cold chain  
5. Sales & distribution  

---

## 1. Fingerling Supply (Hatcheries)

### Role:

- Supply high-quality juvenile fish  
- Ensure genetic quality and disease resistance  
- Maintain stable production cycles  

### Potential Partners:

- Local hatcheries (Iran – Caspian & Persian Gulf regions)  
- International hatcheries (Europe / Mediterranean suppliers)  

### Strategy:

- Long-term supply contracts  
- Potential future investment in in-house hatchery  

---

## 2. Feed Supply

### Role:

- Provide high-quality fish feed  
- Ensure growth performance and feed conversion efficiency  

### Key Consideration:

Feed represents the largest operational cost (50–60% of OPEX)

### Potential Partners:

- Skretting  
- BioMar  
- Aller Aqua  
- Regional feed producers  

### Strategy:

- Bulk purchasing contracts  
- Price stability agreements  
- Optimization of feed conversion ratio (FCR)  

---

## 3. Equipment & Infrastructure

### Components:

- Offshore cages  
- Mooring systems  
- Feeding automation systems  
- Monitoring equipment  

### Potential Partners:

- AKVA Group  
- Selstad  
- ScaleAQ  
- Local marine engineering firms  

### Strategy:

- Proven offshore technology  
- Durable systems for harsh marine conditions  
- Modular expansion capability  

---

## 4. Logistics & Cold Chain

### Role:

- Harvest transport  
- Cold storage  
- Export handling  

### Infrastructure:

- Onshore processing facilities  
- Refrigerated transport  
- Port access  

### Potential Partners:

- Regional logistics companies  
- Cold storage providers  
- Port operators  

### Strategy:

- Efficient harvest-to-market time  
- Reduced product loss  
- Export-ready supply chain  

---

## 5. Sales & Distribution

### Markets:

- Domestic market  
- Regional export (UAE, Qatar, Oman)  
- International export (Europe, Asia)  

### Channels:

- Wholesale distributors  
- Seafood processors  
- Retail supply chains  

### Strategy:

- Long-term off-take agreements  
- Export-focused pricing  
- Premium product positioning  

---

## Strategic Partnerships

The project will collaborate with:

- Technology providers (aquaculture systems)  
- Feed manufacturers  
- Hatcheries  
- Logistics operators  
- Export distributors  

👉 Objective:
Build a stable and efficient ecosystem around production  

---

## Integration Strategy

The project follows a semi-integrated model:

- Core production is managed internally  
- Key inputs are secured via partnerships  
- Selected vertical integration (future phases):  
  - Hatchery  
  - Processing  
  - Export channels  

---

## Risk Consideration

### Supply Chain Risks:

- Feed price volatility  
- Fingerling availability  
- Logistics disruptions  
- Export restrictions  

---

### Mitigation:

- Diversified supplier base  
- Long-term contracts  
- Strategic inventory planning  
- Multi-market sales strategy  

---

## Competitive Advantage

- Access to international suppliers  
- Integration with local infrastructure  
- Export-ready logistics positioning  
- Flexible supply chain structure  

---

## Strategic Conclusion

A strong and well-structured partner network is essential for the success of this aquaculture project.

By combining:

- Reliable suppliers  
- Proven technology partners  
- Efficient logistics systems  

👉 The project ensures stable operations, reduced risk, and scalable growth potential.
