# Operations Plan

## Overview

This document outlines the operational framework of the offshore aquaculture project, focusing on daily activities, resource management, logistics, and system efficiency.

The goal is to ensure:
- Continuous production
- Cost efficiency
- Operational reliability
- Scalable expansion

---

## 1. Operational Structure

The project is divided into three main operational layers:

### 1. Production Layer
- Fish farming operations
- Feeding management
- Growth monitoring

### 2. Technical Layer
- Equipment maintenance
- Cage system integrity
- Energy systems (solar + grid)

### 3. Logistics Layer
- Feed supply
- Harvest transport
- Export handling

---

## 2. Daily Operations

### Feeding Operations
- Automated feeding systems
- Scheduled feeding cycles (2–4 times daily)
- Feed conversion ratio (FCR) monitoring

### Monitoring
- Water quality monitoring (O₂, temperature, salinity)
- Fish health observation
- Cage integrity checks

### Maintenance
- Net cleaning
- Structural inspections
- Mooring system checks

---

## 3. Workforce Structure

### Core Team:

- Project Manager
- Marine Operations Supervisor
- Aquaculture Specialist
- Maintenance Technicians
- Logistics Coordinator
- Security Personnel

### Estimated Workforce (per farm unit):
- 15 – 30 personnel depending on scale

---

## 4. Feeding & Growth Management

### Feeding Strategy:
- High-efficiency commercial feed
- Controlled feeding to reduce waste
- Growth optimization

### Growth Cycle:
- Fingerling → Grow-out → Harvest
- Typical cycle: 6–12 months depending on species

---

## 5. Harvesting Operations

- Scheduled harvesting cycles
- Use of service vessels
- Immediate transfer to processing facilities

Key objective:
- Maintain product freshness
- Reduce post-harvest loss

---

## 6. Logistics & Transport

### Input Logistics:
- Feed delivery via boats or nearby ports
- Equipment transport

### Output Logistics:
- Direct transfer to:
  - Processing facilities
  - Export terminals

---

## 7. Infrastructure Support

- Floating platforms (optional)
- Shore-based support facilities
- Access to nearby ports

---

## 8. Energy & Utilities

Hybrid energy system:

- Grid electricity (if available)
- Solar panels (daytime operations)
- Wind turbines (continuous backup)

---

## 9. Biosecurity & Health Management

- Disease monitoring protocols
- Controlled stocking density
- Regular inspections

---

## 10. Operational Efficiency Drivers

Key factors improving efficiency:

- Proximity to raw material supply
- Access to export routes
- Automated feeding systems
- Modular farm design

---

## 11. Scalability Strategy

The operation is designed to expand in phases:

- Phase 1: Pilot farm
- Phase 2: Regional expansion
- Phase 3: Full-scale industrial farming

Each phase increases:
- Production capacity
- Revenue potential
- Operational complexity

---

## 12. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

- Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR)
- Mortality Rate
- Production Volume
- Cost per ton
- Revenue per cycle

---

## Strategic Conclusion

The operational model is designed for:

- High efficiency
- Controlled costs
- Scalable growth
- Export-oriented production

This ensures the project is not only technically feasible, but operationally sustainable and financially viable.
